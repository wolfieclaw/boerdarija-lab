Slang & Street-Level Darija

Slang is where the language starts sounding alive — but also where learners can get themselves into trouble fastest.

The goal here is not to turn you into a performative slang goblin. It’s to help you recognize common casual speech, understand tone, and know what is safe to borrow versus what should stay in your passive understanding for now.

Understand it first. Use it carefully later.

How to use this page

A lot of slang is useful to understand before it is useful to say. Some expressions are harmless and warm. Others depend heavily on tone, age, social context, or how well people know you.

So the default rule is simple: if something is marked as more casual or playful, great — learn to hear it. Use it later, once it feels natural rather than costume-y.

Rules of thumb

Use freely: casual high-frequency words that are normal and safe, like wakha or daba.

Use carefully: socially loaded words, reactions, compliments, and anything that depends on tone or relationship.

Mostly understand first: harder slang, jokey phrases, and things that might sound brilliant in one mouth and ridiculous in another.
