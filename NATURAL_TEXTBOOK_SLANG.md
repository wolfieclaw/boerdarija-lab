# Natural vs Textbook vs Slang

This is one of the most distinctive product features for BoerDarija Lab.

## Core idea
Most language products only teach:
- what a phrase means

BoerDarija Lab should also teach:
- whether it sounds normal
- whether it sounds too formal
- whether it sounds slangy / playful / risky
- whether it feels Casablanca-natural

That is a much more useful real-world layer.

---

## The 3-layer model

### 1. Textbook
Formal, correct, or understandable — but often not what people naturally say in everyday Casablanca speech.

### 2. Natural
The safest target.
This is what learners should aim for first:
- normal
- usable
- human-sounding
- not stiff

### 3. Slang / clipped / playful
More local, more casual, sometimes more socially charged.
Useful later, but should come with tone warnings.

---

## Why this feature matters
It helps answer questions learners actually have:
- Can I really say this?
- Will I sound weird?
- Is this too formal?
- Is this too casual?
- What would a real person say instead?

That is one of the strongest ways to differentiate this project.

## Example

### Situation: asking where someone is

**Textbook**  
`أين أنت؟`  
`ayna anta?`  
Understandable, but too formal / MSA-like for normal WhatsApp use.

**Natural**  
`فينك؟`  
`fink?`  
Short, normal, and the best target for everyday use.

**Slang / clipped**  
`فينيك دابا؟`  
`finik daba?`  
A bit more pointed / immediate depending on tone.
