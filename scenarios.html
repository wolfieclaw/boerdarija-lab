Real-Life Casablanca Scenarios

This is where BOERDARIJA starts feeling less like a generic course and more like a survival-and-social-fluency tool. Learn useful Darija through practical situations, natural phrasing, and the kind of language you actually need in Casablanca.

The aim is simple: help you understand what people are likely to say, give you safe ways to reply, and gradually show you the more natural and slangy versions too.

Pilot scenario layer — first direction pass

How to use these scenarios

Path 1

Survival first

Start with taxis, cafés, and markets if your goal is simple: function better in Casablanca this week, not in theory someday.

Path 2

Social first

Start with greetings, family/social rhythm, and WhatsApp if your bigger pain is warmth, timing, and not freezing around people.

Path 3

Sound more human

Use the comparison and slang features if you already know some basics and want to stop sounding textbook-shaped.

Pilot scenarios

High-value everyday use

Taxi Basics

Get from frozen foreigner to functional passenger: say where you want to go, ask if the driver knows the place, check rough cost, and avoid sounding like a confused tourist with a wallet.

  • Tell the driver your destination clearly
  • Ask if they know the place
  • Ask about the price without getting weird
  • Say stop here / wait a second / go straight

Fast confidence boost

Café Ordering

One of the quickest ways to feel more normal in Morocco: order tea, coffee, water, or something small without defaulting to awkward survival mime.

  • Order drinks and simple food
  • Ask what’s available
  • Request no sugar / more sugar
  • Ask for the bill naturally

Very Casablanca

Market Bargaining

Not hardcore souk theatre — just enough language to ask the price, react without panic, push back lightly, and leave with dignity still attached.

  • Ask “how much?” naturally
  • Say it’s too expensive
  • Negotiate without sounding robotic
  • Walk away politely when needed
