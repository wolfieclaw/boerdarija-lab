Taxi Basics

One of the fastest ways to feel less lost in Morocco is to handle a taxi ride without defaulting to English, panic, or interpretive hand gestures. This page focuses on the useful Darija that gets you moving, clarifies destination, and keeps the interaction normal.

We’re starting with the safest, most useful lines first. Later this can expand into fuller roleplay, listening drills, and more natural/slangier variants.

Core phrases

These are the first lines worth knowing if you use taxis regularly in Casablanca.

سلام
salam
Hello
safe natural audio ready
Simple, useful, and normal. A small thing, but it changes the tone immediately.
بغيت نمشي ل...
bghit nmshi l-...
I want to go to ...
safe casablanca use audio ready
Now with dedicated scenario audio instead of a rough support line.
لفين؟
l-fin?
To where?
natural high-frequency audio ready
Very useful for hearing and recognizing what the driver is asking.
بشحال؟
bshal?
How much?
safe natural audio ready
Short, essential, and now properly recorded.
قدّام
qddam
Straight ahead / forward

Useful for giving short navigation corrections without overcomplicating things.
على اليمين
3la l-ymin
To the right

Very practical and high value for actual movement through a city.
على اليسار
3la l-ysar
To the left
safe audio ready
Same logic as right — now cleanly recorded too.
وقف هنا، عفاك
wqef hna, 3afak
Stop here, please
safe natural audio ready
One of the most useful full lines in the whole scenario.
بلاتي، تسنّى شوية
belati, tsenna shwiya
Wait a bit
natural audio ready
Great for those tiny moments where you’re getting cash out, checking your phone, or not quite ready to move.
ما فهمتش
ma fhemtsh
I didn’t understand
safe audio ready
A survival superpower. Absolutely worth dedicated audio.
عاود، عفاك
3awed, 3afak

safe audio ready
This now has a proper full-line recording instead of just the single-word support audio.
شكرا
shukran
Thank you
safe audio ready
Never glamorous, always useful.

Mini dialogue

This is the shape of a very normal taxi interaction. It’s intentionally simple.

Driver
سلام، لفين؟
salam, l-fin?
Hi, where to?
You
بغيت نمشي للمعاريف
bghit nmshi l-Maarif
I want to go to Maarif.
Driver
واش قريب ولا بعيد؟
wash qrib wlla b3id?
Is it near or far?
You
قريب من هنا. وقف هنا، عفاك.
qrib mn hna. wqef hna, 3afak.
It’s near here. Stop here, please.
You
شكرا
shukran
Thank you.

Natural vs textbook vs slang-ish

One of the things BoerDarija should eventually do really well is show not just what something means, but how normal it sounds.

textbook
أريد الذهاب إلى المعاريف
urid adhhab ila l-Maarif
Understandable, but much too formal and classroom-ish for a normal taxi interaction.
natural
بغيت نمشي للمعاريف
bghit nmshi l-Maarif
This is the safe, useful version. Normal, practical, and exactly the kind of thing you want first.
clipped / contextual
للمعاريف، عفاك
l-Maarif, 3afak
Shorter and more clipped. Useful later, once you’re comfortable enough not to sound like you’re assembling language out of spare parts.

Quick practice

Mini challenge: imagine the driver asks "l-fin?" What would you say if you wanted to go to Maarif and then stop near a café?

A good simple answer might be:
bghit nmshi l-Maarif ... wqef hna, 3afak.

Not elegant poetry. Just useful survival language. Which, frankly, is a more respectable goal than poetry in a taxi.
